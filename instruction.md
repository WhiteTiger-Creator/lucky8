Network-flow quarantine is a security containment control, and during the Tideguard security incident the rollup that decides which flows require responder quarantine began emitting an untrustworthy queue, leaving malicious flows uncontained. The restored system must reflect the flow-review board's security rulings exactly. A reconciler at /app/workflow/export_report.py must turn the raw flow events into the containment artifacts summary.json, subnet_matrix.json and quarantined.jsonl that responders act on, and an audit tool at /app/flow_audit.py must offer two subcommands, diagnose and repair: diagnose emits a stateless report of the six known containment defects with their evidence, and repair rebuilds the rollup, runs it, and writes diagnosis.json and repair_audit.json beside the three artifacts, defaulting to /app/output. The board's security decisions behind every value are recorded in the dossier at /app/incident/flow_review_dossier.md, the authoritative record where a later dated ruling overrides an earlier one, and the exact schemas, key sets, field types, digest payloads and checksum serialization are fixed by the contract at /app/docs/report_spec.json. The read-only frozen snapshot at /app/workflow/.export_report.original must remain unmodified, and every emitted artifact must validate against the contract.
