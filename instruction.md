You are the incident responder on the Tideguard network-intrusion containment platform during an active breach. The automated control that quarantines hostile network flows — deciding which flows on each subnet are isolated from the fleet and escalated to responders — has been tampered with and is now releasing malicious flows back onto the network instead of containing them. Restoring correct containment before the intruder pivots further is the security objective; the fleet is exposed for as long as the quarantine decisions are wrong.

The tampered control lives at `/app/workflow/export_report.py`. It reads the raw flow-telemetry stream off the sensors and must once again decide containment correctly, leaving three records the responders act on: the incident summary `summary.json`, the per-subnet exposure map `subnet_matrix.json`, and the quarantine queue `quarantined.jsonl`. A triage tool at `/app/flow_audit.py` exposes two actions. `diagnose --dossier PATH --report PATH` reads the review log at the `--dossier` path and **writes** its report of the six known containment defects — with the forensic evidence for each — to the `--report` path, which is an output file it creates (not an input to read). `repair --output-dir PATH` restores the control from its frozen pre-incident snapshot, re-runs containment, and records the containment diagnosis (`diagnosis.json`) and the repair audit (`repair_audit.json`) beside the three records, defaulting to `/app/output`. The repair audit's `forbidden_tokens_removed` lists exactly the spec's `workflow_repair.forbidden_tokens` that were removed — those tokens only, with no additional entries of your own.

Every containment threshold — how trust tiers are ranked, when a hostile flow is isolated, how repeated exposure accumulates against a subnet, and which flows cross into the responder queue — was ruled on by the flow-review board while the incident was live, and those rulings survive only in the review dossier at `/app/incident/flow_review_dossier.md`, where a later dated ruling supersedes an earlier one. Most of that log is routine incident chatter, so each threshold must be traced to its final ruling rather than the first mention. The exact record and digest formats the responders' tooling validates against are pinned in `/app/docs/report_spec.json`. The frozen pre-incident snapshot at `/app/workflow/.export_report.original` is read-only forensic evidence and must not be altered. Containment is judged on behaviour: it must hold against an alternate flow-telemetry capture it has never seen, reach identical decisions on repeated runs, and derive every containment call from the telemetry and the board's rulings alone.
