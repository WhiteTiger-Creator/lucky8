The Vantor sensor bus authenticates every telemetry frame with a keyed integrity tag, and the decoder is the trust boundary that must reject any frame that fails that tag or is otherwise malformed. After a firmware update the codec at `/app/codec.py` no longer applies the bus keying or the control-byte escaping — so conforming devices reject its output, and, more seriously, its decoder no longer enforces the frame's integrity and structural rules, leaving it unable to tell a genuine frame from a tampered or forged one.

`/app/docs/frame_spec.md` is the authoritative VTF-1 frame structure and integrity contract — the frame layout, the body fields, and the decode validation order. It deliberately does **not** fix the specific bus constants: how the integrity tag is keyed, which bytes it covers, its byte order on the wire, the escape mask used by byte-stuffing, and how the `FLAGS` parity bit is computed. Those were settled over the bus bring-up and their final values are recorded in `/app/docs/bus_bringup_log.md`. That log is a working record — the February bench drafts were revised during the May bus review, and several first-pass values (unkeyed tag, payload-only coverage, little-endian order, the `0x20` escape mask, length-based parity) were reversed. Read it as an account that resolves over time: where a February draft and a later bring-up decision disagree, the later decision governs. A single wrong constant produces frames that no conforming device will accept.

Restore `/app/codec.py` so that `encode(payload: bytes) -> bytes` produces byte-identical, correctly keyed and escaped frames, and `decode(frame: bytes) -> bytes` fully validates every frame — recomputing the keyed integrity tag over the calibrated coverage, checking the declared length, the parity bit, the reserved bits, and the delimiters — and raises `FrameError` on any frame that violates the contract, whether the corruption is a tampered integrity tag, a forged length, a bad parity flag, or a broken delimiter. Keep the module importable and keep the existing `encode`/`decode`/`FrameError` names and the command-line interface intact.

`/app/samples/vectors.json` holds a few smoke-test payloads: round-trip each through your codec (`decode(encode(p))` must return `p`). They carry no expected frame bytes — the wire format is fixed by the spec and the final bring-up constants, so derive the frames from those rather than reverse-engineering them from examples. Do not read or import anything from `/tests` or `/solution`.
