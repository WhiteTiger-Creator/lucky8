The Vantor sensor bus authenticates every telemetry frame with a keyed integrity tag, and the decoder is the trust boundary that must reject any frame that fails that tag or is otherwise malformed. After a firmware update the codec at `/app/codec.py` no longer applies the bus keying or the control-byte escaping — so conforming devices reject its output, and, more seriously, its decoder no longer enforces the frame's integrity and structural rules, leaving it unable to tell a genuine frame from a tampered or forged one.

`/app/docs/frame_spec.md` is the authoritative VTF-1 wire format and integrity contract. Restore `/app/codec.py` so that `encode(payload: bytes) -> bytes` produces byte-identical, correctly keyed and escaped frames, and `decode(frame: bytes) -> bytes` fully validates every frame — recomputing the keyed integrity tag over the body, checking the declared length, the parity bit, the reserved bits, and the delimiters — and raises `FrameError` on any frame that violates the contract, whether the corruption is a tampered integrity tag, a forged length, a bad parity flag, or a broken delimiter. Keep the module importable and keep the existing `encode`/`decode`/`FrameError` names and the command-line interface intact.

A handful of known-good `payload -> frame` pairs are in `/app/samples/vectors.json` to check against. Do not read or import anything from `/tests` or `/solution`.
